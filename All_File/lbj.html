Who is Lebron James?

LeBron Raymone James Sr., more commonly known as LeBron James was born on December 30, 1984. He is an American professional basketball player who currently plays for the Los Angeles Lakers of the National Basketball Associaton. James is widely considered one of the greatest players of all time and he is referred to by many fans as "King James". In high school, James played basketball for St. Vincent-St.Mary High School in Akron, Ohio, his hometown. He became the first overall pick in the 2003 NBA draft and began his professional career playing for Cleveland Cavaliers. Later in 2004, he was named NBA Rookie of the Year. James went on to establish himself as one of the league's star players and won the NBA MVP award consecutively in 2009 and 2010. However, he left Cleveland in 2010 as a free agent to join the Miami Heat in an effort to reach the championship trophy. He won his first two NBA championships while playing for the Miami Heat in 2012 and 2013 where he also earned the league's MVP and Finals MVP awards. LeBron later returned to Cleveland in 2014 to lead the team to winning its first NBA Championship in 2016 after a come back from a 3-1 deficit against the Golden State Warriors. Most recently, he won his fourth championship in 2020, two years after signing with the Los Angeles Lakers. With all of his accomplishments, James will remain one of the greatest players in the history of basketball.
