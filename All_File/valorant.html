All About Valorant:

What is Valorant?

Valorant is a 5 vs 5 character-based tactical First-Person Shooter (FPS) developed and published by Riot Games which released on June 2, 2020. The game development began in 2014 and it was first teased on October 2019 under the name Project A. Therefore, Valorant is a fairly new game and is currently being played a lot by many new people who are still discovering it. Valorant is inspired by the game Counter-Strike series of tactical shooters with many similar features such as the buy menu, spray patterns and inaccuracy while moving. Users play in the role of “agents” in a type of near-future dystopian world, each having a unique set of abilities. There are currently 19 agents as off the latest game update. Every agent has common abilities as well as their ultimate which they get once they have killed enough people to fill the ultimate bar. Players also have a wide selection of guns to choose from as there are 17 different guns available to every player.

Valorant Game Objective:

The game's "competitive" mode as well as "unrated" mode is 5 vs 5 with one team playing the role of attackers and the other the role of defenders. The first side to win 13 rounds will win the whole match (best of 25). The attackers has a bomb-type device called the Spike and their goal is to plant and detonate it at one of two (or three) sites on the battlefield. The defender’s objective is to prevent the spike from detonating by defusing it if it has been planted. If the defender fails to defuse the spike (bomb) 45 seconds after it has been planted, they would lose the round. However, both teams can also win the round if they eliminate the enemy (kill all 5 players on the other team). Yet, for defenders even if they kill all 5 players, if the spike was already planted then they will have to defuse to claim victory.


Other Game Modes:

  • Unrated: first team to win 13 rounds as explained above.

  • Spike Rush: features a shorter game of only first to 4 rounds wins. Each round, a specific gun is assigned to everyone in the game, so that everyone is playing with the same gun.

  • Deathmatch: there are no abilities and it is simply free gunplay with 14 players put on a map, and the first to 40 kills wins.

  • Replication: similar to regular but it is only first to 5 wins. However, the catch with this one is that everyone on your team must play with the same agent.

  • Competitive: similar to regular where the first team that reaches 13 wins. However, it determines your ranking against other players depending on how many matches you win per season.
